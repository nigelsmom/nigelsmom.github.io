Who Is She?

In this brief Q&A, Sweetlenox.com's founder Karen Peterson talks about the inspiration and purpose behind her very young, successful blog, the challenges of advocacey in her first year and what the future might hold in 2017 for Sweetlenox.com and the brand.

Why did you create the blog Sweetlenox.com?

After being diagnosed with Stage 1 Triple Negative Breast Cancer in February 2015, I went thru a period of challenges that effected my life dramtically. Although I put together one of the best medical teams in NYC, I still experienced a deep learning curve. Understanding how to manage a chronic diagnosis such as breast cancer is an enormous undertaking, and without education, guidance and support it can be extremely confusing and islolating. I created Sweetlenox.com as a two fold entity. Part one a.k.a. "The Believers" section is chock full of interviews and information highlighting providers who are truly passionate about their field. Whether it be artists covering scars, patient navigators getting bills paid or biologist talking about the latest clinical trails, I'm putting the spotlight on the brightest and the best!

The second part of Sweetlenox.com is my personal blog, detailing my journey over the last year (going thru treatment) and chronicles some of the wonderful folks I've met along the way. Throughout 2016 I've had the honor of chatting it up with and rubbing shoulders with some of the the most passionate people in art, biology, fashion, urban farming, nutrition, and breast cancer advocacey. All of these special people have one thing in common: To make live more accesible, creative, fun and managable for Breast Cancer Survivors!

So What does the future hold for Sweetlenox.com?

My goals are to addres these critical areas:
  1. Improve the look and function of my blog
  2. Become comfortable with cross marketing on social media accounts
  3. Become more vocal on social media
  4. Form a 501.3c and have a grass roots fundraiser
  5. Expand my interviews with providers outside of NYC
  6. Find a mentor, someone who can continue to help me grow
